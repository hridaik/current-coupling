# Phase 4B.6 — Mammalian Attention Comparison
Date: 2026-06-12

---

## Condition for This Section

Per Phase 4B authorization:
> "Discuss only if [alignment enrichment] is supported."

**Alignment enrichment was NOT detected.** The candidate interpretation as stated
in the authorization — that worm dwelling "creates aligned state-dependent organization"
analogous to how attention removes aligned shared variability — is NOT supported by
the data.

The proposed symmetry does not hold for this dataset:
- Attention (mammalian): reduces shared variability among same-encoding neurons → improves decoding
- Worm dwelling (proposed): creates shared variability among same-encoding neurons → degrades decoding

For this symmetry to be applicable, PDF-connected pairs would need to have same-direction
behavioral encoding. They do not.

---

## What the Data Actually Shows: A Different Symmetry

While the attention-based alignment symmetry does not apply, the data suggests a
different and biologically interesting structure:

**The PDF-associated conditional organization during dwelling preferentially connects
neurons with COMPLEMENTARY behavioral encoding.**

The three primary PDF pairs by |ΔQ|:
- ADEL (velocity-positive, roaming-active) ↔ URYVR (velocity-negative, dwelling-active)
- ADEL (velocity-positive) ↔ URYDL (near-zero velocity encoding, high pumping)
- ADEL (velocity-positive) ↔ RMEL (weakly velocity-positive, high pumping)

ADEL is a mechanosensory neuron (dopaminergic, anterior deirid) that activates during
forward locomotion. URYVR/URYDL are O2/social signal integrators that modulate locomotion
state. The conditional dependence between these neurons emerging preferentially during
dwelling may reflect a **cross-variable coordination** function:

- ADEL reports mechanosensory contact history (touching food)
- URY neurons report O2 levels and social signals (proximity to other worms)
- During dwelling: both signals are behaviorally relevant (animal is exploiting a food patch)
- During roaming: neither signal is as useful for state maintenance (animal is searching)

This would be a form of **multi-sensory integration** that becomes conditionally organized
during the state when multiple sensory inputs converge on the same behavioral decision
(stay on food or leave). This is distinct from the attention/information-limiting framework,
which concerns same-variable encoding.

---

## Conclusion

The attention literature comparison proposed in the Phase 4B authorization is not supported
by the data. No alignment enrichment is present.

A different observation emerges: PDF-associated dwelling-state conditional organization
preferentially connects neurons with complementary (anti-aligned) behavioral encoding,
which is more consistent with a cross-variable coordination function than with an
information-limiting or attention-like mechanism.

**This alternative interpretation is speculative** and not tested quantitatively in this
phase. It is recorded here as a hypothesis for future experimental evaluation.
